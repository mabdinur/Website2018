Hi, my name is Munir Abdinur and I am a second year Systems Design Engineering student with a keen interest in web development, data engineering and project manangement.

So far I have completed software engineering internships at Initium Labs, Toronto Transit Commision and Brisk Synergies where I honed my skills in fullstack web development, data engineering and user centered design.

At Initium Labs I helped create a routing and carrier selection application for Sears Canada which determined optimal shipping routes for online orders. Halfway through my coop term, I was also tasked with writing an emergency script to recover over 1000 orders which were dropped the course of the past 2 weeks, saving Sears Canada tens of thousands of dollars in revenue.

At the Toronto Transit Commission I developed a desktop application to standardise all TTC travel alert across social media, bus terminals, and train stations. I created a user friendly data entry interface for transit control using VBA, Access and Excel Forms. The goal was to automate the generation of alerts to reduce human error and increase consistency of travel alerts. Nearing the end of my coop term I analysed TTC subway logs and developed a new recommendation algorithm which are set to improve the accuracy of train predictions by over 30%.

At Brisk Synergies I used traffic footage from various municipalities and transit organisation to analyse near miss conflicts at high traffic intersections. With the help of my highly specialised team we were able to identify key risk indicators using types of road users, vehicle/pedestrian speeds and conflict angles. As a data engineer and full-stack development intern, I also designed the analytics dashboard using Ruby on Rails, Bootstrap and Google Charts as well as configured our ETL pipelines and RESTful API to handle new traffic metrics such as our patent pending safety index.
